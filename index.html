Dr. Eric Eustace Williams

1st Prime Minister of Trinidad and Tobago

He served as prime minister from 1962 until his death. He was also a noted Caribbean historian, and is widely regarded as "The Father of The Nation."

Here's the story of "The Father of a Nation"

  • 1911 - Born in Trinidad and Tobago WI to Thomas Henry Williams a minor civil servant and Eliza Frances Boissiere a descendant of the French Creole elite. Educated at Queen's Royal College in Port of Spain
  • 1932 - He was awarded an island scholarship which allowed him to attend St Catherine's society, Oxford (which became St Catherine's College, Oxford).
  • 1935 - Received first-class honours for his B.A. in history, and was ranked in first place among University of Oxford students grudating in history. He also represented the soccer team.
  • 1938 - Completes doctorate. In his autobiography, Inward Hunger, he expresses his experience of racism in Great Britain, and the effects on him during his travels in Germany after the Nazi seizure of power.
  • 1944 - Dr. williams was appointed to the Anglo-Averican Carribean Commission.
  • 1948 - Returned To Trinidad as the Commission's Deputy Chairman of the Carribbean research council. Back in his homeland, he delivers a series of educational lectures, for which he becomes famous.
  • 1950 - Manuscript on Education in the British West Indies remained unpublished for years until he published it in Trinidad.
  • 1955 - In disagreements between Dr. Williams the Commission decided not to renew his contract.
  • 1956 - Inaugurated his own political party, The People's National Movement (PNM)
  • 1962 - His party would usher Trinidad and Tobago into independence, and dominate its post-colonial politics. The PNM’s first document was its constitution. Unlike the other political parties of the time, the PNM was a highly organized, hierarchical body. Its second document was The People’s Charter, in which the party strove to separate itself from the transitory political assemblages which had been the norm in Trinidad's politics.
  • 1981 - Passes away at the age of 69.

"Williams sent one of 73 Apollo 11 Goodwill Messages to NASA for the historic first lunar landing in 1969. The message still rests on the lunar surface today. He wrote, in part: "It is our earnest hope for mankind that while we gain the moon, we shall not lose the world."
